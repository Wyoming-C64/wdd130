You are given four random digits ranging from 1 through 9. Your goal is to use arithmetic operations (add, subtract, multiply, and divide) along with parentheses to make the resulting value equal 24.

The ZX Spectrum solution takes advantage of a feature not available on the Commodore 64: the VAL() function—which is used to convert numbers in strings into actual numeric values in Spectrum's BASIC is able to parse mathmaetcal expressions in addition to digits found within a string. The Commodore BASIC VAL() function looks only for numeric expressions, and the instant it encounters a non-digit character, it quits evaluating the string.After much thought, I decided to get the C64's BASIC interpreter to do the work of evaluating the expression. but I found my own solution that I thought was pretty clever, and this became my first self-modifying BASIC program.
